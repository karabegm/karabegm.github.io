Enchiladas Blessed by the Gods

Originally a Mexican street food, enchiladas began simply as rolled tortillas dipped in chili sauce. As Mexican immigrants moved into Texas, also known as Tejanos, they brought their culinary traditions with them, adapting them along the way to create the Tex-Mex cuisine we know and love today. These enchiladas fall somewhere in between, but they're definitely Tex-Mex at their core. They may not be authentic by, well, anyone's standards, but they're quick, easy, filling, and delicious.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 yellow onion, chopped
  • Kosher salt
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 20 oz enchilada sauce
  • 8 oz canned crushed tomatoes
  • 3 c. cooked, shredded chicken
  • 1/4 cup chopped cilantro
  • 8 oz shredded cheddar cheese
  • 8 oz shredded Monterey Jack Cheese
  • flour tortillas, warmed

Directions:

  1. Preheat oven to 350. In a food processor, pulse garlic, bell pepper, and onion until finely chopped.
  2. Heat a large skillet over medium high heat with oil. Add onion mixture and fry until softened, 6-8 min. Add seasoning.
  3. Stir in enchilada sauce and crushed tomatoes and let cook, stirring occassionally. Reserve 3/4 cup of sauce mixture for topping enchiladas.
  4. In a medium bowl, combine chicken, cilantro, 1 c. Cheddar, and 1 c. Monteray Jack. Add 1 cup of sauce mixture and mix to combine. Season as necessary.
  5. Spread remaining sauce mixture in the bottom of a 13x9 baking dish
  6. Spoon mixture into center of warm tortillas and roll. Arrange tortilla rolls in baking dish.
  7. Spread extra sauce on top of tortillas and sprinkle with remaining cheese.
  8. Bake until cheese is melted, 15-20 min.

    9. Serve with sour cream, cilantro, and lime wedges as toppings.
    Enjoy!
